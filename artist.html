About Ellie

Ellie is a 24-year-old singer/songwriter in Portsmouth, NH. She grew up in Central Mississippi, where she fell in love with slow living and country music. After high school, she moved to Nashville, Tennessee, where she worked as a performing musician, songwriter, and session instrument player for five years. During that time, she came out as transgender and began to question her safety in the South. In 2024, she moved to Portsmouth, NH and found a new home in an accepting place where she could finally pursue the life she had always dreamt of living: writing and recording her own music, surrounded by good friends, just a few minutes from the beach.

Ellie's Music

Ellie is a singer/songwriter whose music feels like a late-night conversation where vulnerability and stories collide. Influenced by artists like Jim Croce, Jason Isbell, and Lizzy McAlpine, her stripped-down acoustic sound thrives on raw emotion and rich harmonies. Each song is an intimate experience, focusing on the connection between melody and meaning. Ellie’s music invites listeners to pause, reflect, and connect. Her lyrics delve into the anxieties that keep us up at night—whether it's the overwhelming weight of climate change and panic attacks in “Touch the Moon” or the quiet frustration of small-town life in “Real World.” Through it all, her humor weaves a little warmth into every song, reminding us that sometimes, the best way to cope is to sing about it.
